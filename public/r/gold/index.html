About rhyming with "gold"

"Gold" is one of the most-used words in songwriting because it carries weight on its own — wealth, value, perfection, longing — and lands on a hard D that gives a satisfying close to a line. From "Heart of Gold" (Neil Young) to "Fields of Gold" (Sting) to "Pure Gold" (Earth, Wind & Fire), the -old ending threads through every era of popular music.

Strict rhymes for "gold" are limited — only 14 single words share the exact -ohld ending. To open up your options, try the near-rhyme groups above: dropping the D to land on -ole (bowl, soul, whole) gives a softer close, while swapping the consonant cluster to -one lets you stretch into longer thematic words like "alone" and "thrown."
